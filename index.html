ABOUT THE PROJECT
Delhi has been ranked as one of the most polluted cities in the world for several years, and air pollution has become a major health concern for the residents. The high levels of pollution in the city are caused by a combination of factors, including vehicular emissions, industrial activities, and dust and smoke from construction sites. The situation is exacerbated by geographical factors such as the city's location in a bowl-shaped valley, which can trap pollutants and make the air quality worse.

The poor air quality has a significant impact on public health. Exposure to high levels of air pollution can lead to respiratory diseases, heart disease, and other health issues. The World Health Organization estimates that air pollution contributes to around 7 million premature deaths globally each year. Therefore, it is essential to understand the levels and sources of air pollution in Delhi to develop effective strategies to mitigate its impacts on public health.

The air pollution has economic implications as well. It can have a negative impact on tourism, with visitors choosing to avoid areas with high levels of pollution. Additionally, air pollution can lead to increased healthcare costs and reduced productivity due to sickness and absenteeism.

Finally, an analytical project on air quality in Delhi can help raise awareness of the issue and encourage policymakers to take action to improve air quality. It can provide data-driven insights on the sources of pollution and the effectiveness of different interventions, leading to evidence-based policy decisions. Ultimately, this could help reduce the levels of pollution in Delhi and improve the health and wellbeing of its residents.
